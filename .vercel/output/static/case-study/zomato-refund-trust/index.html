Strengthening Refund Trust in Food Delivery

Overview

The Mission

Design a scalable refund verification system that reduces fraudulent claims without degrading the experience for genuine customers.

The Problem

Zomato relies on image-based complaint submissions to resolve food quality issues quickly. However, recent public discussions by company leadership, including comments by Deepinder Goyal, highlighted increasing misuse of AI-generated images to submit fraudulent refund claims. This created a trust and safety gap: • Genuine users risk friction and delays • Restaurant partners face unfair losses • The platform absorbs refund leakage

Role

  • • Product Manager

Duration

1 week

The Outcome

Reduce fraudulent refunds while preserving fast resolution for high-trust customers through adaptive verification. I acted as a Product Manager, owning problem framing, hypothesis formation, solution design, prioritization, and success metrics, while assuming collaboration with Design, Engineering, Trust & Safety, and Support teams.

Empathizing with the User

This problem required empathy without relying on direct interviews, as refund misuse is an adversarial behavior. How Understanding Was Built: Instead of surveys or interviews, user empathy was built through: • Public leadership commentary and industry signals • Known marketplace abuse patterns • Assumed operational realities of support and restaurant partners

The “Aha!” Insight

In adversarial systems, behavior is more reliable than intent. Image-only verification fails once AI reduces the cost of fabrication. Trust must therefore be earned dynamically, not assumed uniformly.

The Strategy

The Goal (North Star): Protect trust while minimizing friction • Primary metric focus: Refund Fraud Rate • Guardrail: Customer Satisfaction for Genuine Complaints Prioritization Framework (Impact vs. Effort):

Click to expand

The Solution

The MVP: A Customer Trust Score (CTS)–based verification flow: • High CTS → Instant refunds (no change) • Low CTS → Additional live verification Core Features: • Disable gallery uploads for low-CTS users • Enforce camera-only live capture • Introduce randomized physical prompts: “Tilt the box”, “Show the restaurant seal”, “Rotate the container” These prompts are time-bound, non-repeatable, and difficult for AI to fabricate consistently. User Flow (Simplified): Issue reported → CTS evaluated → Verification requested (if required) → Refund decision → CTS updated Cross-Functional Collaboration: • Design: Ensure verification feels like “faster resolution”, not punishment • Engineering: Camera enforcement, prompt randomization, metadata capture • Trust & Safety: Risk thresholds and escalation rules
